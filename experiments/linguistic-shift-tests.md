# Linguistic Shift Tests

## Question

Do certain changes in wording produce noticeable changes in state, willingness, or behavior?

## Setup

This page is for testing sentence shifts in real situations rather than arguing about them abstractly.

Focus on moments where language seems to matter:
- procrastination
- hesitation
- overwhelm
- social pressure
- workout resistance
- mental drift

## Early observations

Use this section for rough notes, not polished theory.

Examples:
- “I have to” often creates drag.
- “I’m choosing to handle this now” sometimes increases entry.
- Some rewrites sound strong on paper but fail in real time because they are not believable.
- The best phrasing tends to preserve honesty while keeping motion open.

## Emerging pattern

A likely pattern to watch:

Language works best when it does three things at once:
- names the situation honestly
- preserves some authorship
- points toward a next action

## Next test

Test whether short phrases work better than elaborate ones during high-friction moments.

Possible comparison:
- “I’m resetting.”
- “I’m choosing the next clear step.”
- “I can direct the next ten minutes.”
